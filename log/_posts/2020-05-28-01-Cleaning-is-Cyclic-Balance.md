### Cleaning

Everything has its space. Cleaning is an act of giving everything its place. Through Cleaning, we connect the objects with their appropriate place. It is similar to bringing back everyone to their home. While the pandemic has caused significant difficulties, grief and pain, there are some silver linings to look at. The pandemic has forced us to not just be clean individually but also brought many people back to their homes. This itself is an act of cleaning at the scale of the globe. Cleaning does not isolate the dust from that which is pure (not dust), but instead, it temporarily gives the dust its own space to settle. Cleaning identifies the place of dust to be going away from light towards the other end of the spectrum for some time, only to spring back and eventually come back to light in a good form. That is, Cleaning temporarily lets the dust go to its own place and eventually allows it to come back in a renewed form. Thus Cleaning is a Balancing act. Cleaning balances the right amount of things to keep in light and the left amount is sent to the dark to rest for a while. Thus, we see Cleaning is Cyclic Balance.


### Identifying myself as a Cleaner

I myself never gave much attention to physical cleaning of non-academic objects that belonged to me. During the pandemic, my attention went towards these belongings and I started to appreciate how much energy is required in cleaning. Learning to clean taught me a great deal about what I had missed to see. Much of the root level academic insights came from the clarity obtained through cleaning. Cleaning physical objects also cleans our minds. When we clean physical objects, we start seeing our own reflection clearly. This enables us to identify the outer with the inner. Meeting of the two parts is where there is equilibrium. Hence, Mirrors should be clear and kept clean. 

While cleaning my bathroom I would initially feel bad to look at dirt and push it down the drain. As I kept Cleaning day by day I slowly started to accept dirt and its temporary place down the drain. Instead of cleaning my room by a broom, I started picking up fallen pieces of my hair and dust by my own hands and legs. Cleaning properly requires a lot of energy to pay attention to details and maintain efficiency in technique. I identified myself completely as a Cleaner and my academic pursuits to be acts of Cleaning. Mathematics, Physics and Engineering is all a process of Cleaning. This identification made me cry for a long time since I missed to notice such a simple thing which was right in front of me since childhood. This also enabled me to look at the great deal of Cleaning that our parents and other elders do with a lot of respect. We are all cleaners. In different professions and at different degrees within a profession, Cleaning takes different forms. In Bicycling, we must give equal space to both left and right hand side and alternate equally between them to move straight ahead while maintaining balance. In Acting, the actor must clean his physical and mental space to allow for the given Character to take form. All new things require making room. We let the old which has had its time to compress and go temporarily for rest while new is allowed to take its space. This process was identified to be a cycle when I realized that it is the old which gives rise to the new. It is the old waste which gets recycled in various ways through the processes on Earth that we get to eat food and convert it to new forms. The Food cycle and the Water Cycle are examples of Cleaning. Our own body is a Cleaning process. This is how to Identify Cleaning as a Mass Balance. There are different forms of Mass. Together everything constitues Identity. Hence all mass Identified together is conserved to Identity. However, the forms of mass keep oscillating like phases that come one by one. This is what makes Mass Balance fundamental.

### The Universe Cleans in Cycles

Light does the act of Cleaning in the Universe. The Sun is the source which provides light to clean. Daylight provides space for pure to come together. NightLight provides space for the dust to settle. Day is full of energy and Night is rest. Rest allows time to renew. Thus, Day and night are a cleaning act (mass balance). Different people have different schedules (scales) which determine their own balance. Some people like to have a lot more time awake and stay active much longer than the amount for which they sleep. 

The Water Cycle shows how important it is to purify something before it is put to temporary rest. The Natural Process of evaporation and condensation makes sure that the water is pure (distilled) before coming down as rain. We must also make sure that the waste that we put in for recycling should be purified before it is comperessed.

The food cycle shows how the top of the Food chain depends heavily on the bottom to be able to continue the cycle and survive. 

Sun is the Source of light and a Black Hole is the sink (the other end where dust is placed temporarily). Hence the black hole is Logarithmic and acts as a springboard for time. 

Mahabharata can be seen as an act of Cleaning. To keep the balance, one must be able to look at both sides: the pure and the dust with the same eyes. Thus, Krishna had to stay out of it, neither taking part in one side nor the other. It is the same with a mirror that maintains balance between the reflections and hence its in between the two.  

Farming is Cleaning. Sowing seeds in the mud, followed by water, light and food to let a plant grow involves a tremendous amount of balance. For the body of the plant itself, growing upwards towards the Light provided by Sun requires growing roots away from light down in the mud. The body of the plant itself displays Cleaning as a Balance. 

Breathing is Cleaning. Breathing in allows air to enter and breathing out lets the exhaust to be let out. Breathing in and out in cycles isthe Balance in Cleaning. Similarly, Heart beats set the Circadian cycle by pumping in and out alternatively to keep balance.
