### Equivariant Schubert Calculus
- Equivariant Cohomology
  - Space with symmetry (action of group) then invariance which accounts for symmetry (action).
    - $$EG$$ contractible space with free G-Action. The quotient space $$EG \times X$$ identifying action points. Cohomology of this space.
      - Example: Let $$G= \mathbb{C*}$$. Infinite sphere is contractible.
  - Approximate infinite by finite.
  - Properties shared with ordinary cohomology.
    - Functorial for equivariant maps. 
  - Unique to Equivariant setting
    - Restricts to ordinary cohomology.
    - Determined at fixed locus.
      - Torus: invariant $$k$$-dimensional subspaces are generated by basis choose $$k$$.
 - Equivariant Schubert Class
   - Schubert Cell with position indexed by $$k$$ element subset is T-invariant subvariety.
