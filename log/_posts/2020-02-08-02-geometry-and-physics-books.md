### Books on Geometry and Physics
Interplay of Geometry and Physics has always been a fascinating area of study, right from optics to classical mechanics and general relativity. Partial Differential Equations that form the language in which Laws of Physics are written are hence naturally intertwined with geometry. Here are listed some resources to get started with in this area:
* [Theodore Frankel's The Geometry of Physics](https://www.amazon.in/Geometry-Physics-Introduction-Theodore-Frankel/dp/1107602602)
* [Nakahara's Geometry, Topology and Physics](https://www.amazon.com/Geometry-Topology-Physics-Graduate-Student/dp/0750306068)
* [Levi-Civita's The Absolute Differential Calculus](https://www.amazon.com/Absolute-Differential-Calculus-Tensors-Mathematics/dp/0486634019)
* [Chris Isham's Modern differential geometry for physicists](https://www.amazon.com/Differential-Geometry-Physicists-Scientific-Lecture/dp/9810235623)
* [Jurgen Jost's Geometry and Physics](https://www.springer.com/gp/book/9783642005404)
* [Jerry Kazdan's Applications of Partial Differential Equations To Problems in Geometry](https://www.math.upenn.edu/~kazdan/japan/japan.pdf)
