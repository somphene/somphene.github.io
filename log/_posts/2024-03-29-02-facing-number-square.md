### New Look at the Square Question
Find a number such that squaring it gives $$4$$

Please may we consider the following
$$
\begin{array} {|c|c|}
\hline 
  Standard  & New  \\ 
\hline
 \textrm{Let there be}\,\, x &   \textrm{Please may we offer our best}\,\, 44\\ 
\hline 
 x^2 =4 & 44^2 =4 \\ 
\hline
 x^2-4 =4-4  &  44^2-4 =4-4\\
 \hline
 (x +2)(x-2)= 0  & (44 +2)(44-2)= 0 \\
 \hline
 x +2 =0 \,\,\textrm{or} x-2= 0 &  44 +2 =0 \,\,\textrm{or} 44-2= 0 \\
 \hline
 x = 2  \,\,\textrm{or} x=-2  & 2  \,\,\textrm{or} -2\,\, \textrm{is the exchange which makes the equality}\\
\end{array}
$$ 

### Positivity Number

Please may we consider the positivity of numbers introduced in inner and outer number language.
$$
\begin{array} {|c|c|}
\hline 
  Standard  & New  \\ 
\hline
 \textrm{Let there be}\,\, x &   \textrm{Please may we offer our best}\,\, 44\\ 
\hline 
 x^2 =4 & 44^2 =4 \\ 
\hline
 x^2-4 =4-4  &  44^2=2^2\\
 \hline
 (x +2)(x-2)= 0  & 44 = 2 \,\, | ^2 \,\,\textrm{Celebrating similarity of squaring on both sides} \\
 \hline
 x +2 =0 \,\,\textrm{or} x-2= 0 &   \\
 \hline
 x = 2  \,\,\textrm{or} x=-2  & 2  \,\, \textrm{is the exchange which makes the equality}\\
\end{array}
$$ 

Thus we find the only soultion to be positive $$2$$ as the inner number.
