### Liquid Phase Sintering

* Find the $$4^{th}$$ lecture notes linked: [Advanced Ceramics Lecture Notes 4](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1-stbwRzSRnpzLByfw5M3OWdL_8C6mU3q/view?usp=sharing) (Google Drive Link), [Lecture Notes 4](https://github.com/somphene/Advanced-Ceramics-MM-652-IITB-/blob/master/Advanced_Ceramics_Som_S__Phene%20(4).pdf)(Github Link)
* [Wettability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wetting)- Find the derivations for Young's relation and other related models linked : [Young's relation](https://www.intechopen.com/books/surface-energy/re-derivation-of-young-s-equation-wenzel-equation-and-cassie-baxter-equation-based-on-energy-minimiz) 
* Stages: including Rearrangement, Densification and Ostwald Ripening.
* Microstructure of liquid phase sintered material is more rounded by enhanced diffusivity. It may also lead to the reverse case if the liquid content is low: some regions gain boosted growth and others don't. 
* Driving force: surface energy minimization. Application of external pressure helps not only in increasing driving force but also in kinetics.
